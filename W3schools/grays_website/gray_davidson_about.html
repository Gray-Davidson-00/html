Gray Graduates Reed College

Five years is a long time to spend in one place, and it feels far longer when spent at a place like Reed College. I'll be returning to the school in the fall for a spell to work in the Sensation, Cognition, Attention, Language and Perception EEG lab (SCALP) in which I conducted my psychology thesis. I both love and hate Reed right now, and would like to balance that equation toward the former. To that end, I'm travelling across the country for the summer, thinking of nothing academic, and attempting to moult that set of cunning mental disorders-obsessive compulsive traits mostly-which helped me survive such an intense education, but which have now served their purpose and must be discarded.

The Renn-Fayre Planetarium

Reed College's Renn-Fayre is not like most festivals of the genre, it is a yearly celebration of epic proportions, from a face-melting fireworks display, to a 72 hour rave called Blacklodge, to a myriad interactive art projects, the campus is transformed on the first weekend of May into a wonderland worthy of Lewis Caroll's demented inventions (I've actually met a 10 ft. tall hookah-smoking caterpillar). In May 2011, and revitalized a year later, Kara Sowles and I constructed an epic art project. It was made of papier-mache and electricity, specifically a 750 watt stage lightbulb (which eventually set the whole thing on fire), which case beams of light throughout a smokefilled room. Ambient music complete the scene, and the project was dubbed a huge success.

Garnish 2012 Fire Conclave Video

One of two performing groups I'm a part of in Portland is called Garnish, (The Original Black Rock City Garnish). Each spring we train intensely, combining our skills with fire manipulation and circus acrobalance to create a 20 minute production. We film this production in one continuous take, and submit the video to the Burning Man fire council where we compete against 40 other such troupes for a place in the massive burn circle Saturday of Burning Man. Our video submission for 2012 was better than ever, filmed in an alley between warehouses after two nights of getting chased by cops and rampant fire marshals. The costume theme was solid white with buckles, alluding to straight jackets, and the crew is about 20 people large.
